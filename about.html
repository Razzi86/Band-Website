Mt. Joy

ABOUT

Mt. Joy is an American indie rock band formed in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania in 2015. The band is composed of Matt Quinn (vocals, guitar), Sam Cooper (guitar, vocals), Jackie Miclau (bass), Sotiris Eliopoulos (drums), and Michael Byrnes (guitar, vocals). The band's name is derived from a mountain in Valley Forge National Historical Park. Mt. Joy gained popularity with the release of their self-titled debut album in 2018, which includes the hit single "Silver Lining". The band has released several other albums and EPs, including "Rearrange Us" (2020) and "The Fires EP" (2021). The band's name was inspired by a mountain in Valley Forge National Historical Park that Quinn used to hike as a child.

In 2016, Mt. Joy released their self-titled debut EP, which included the single "Astrovan." The song received significant radio airplay and helped the band gain a dedicated fanbase. In 2018, Mt. Joy released their debut album, "Mt. Joy," which included the hit single "Silver Lining." The album received positive reviews and helped solidify the band's place in the indie rock scene. Since their inception, Mt. Joy has toured extensively and played at numerous music festivals, including Bonnaroo, Lollapalooza, and Austin City Limits. In addition to Quinn and Cooper, the band currently consists of Michael Byrnes (bass), Sotiris Eliopoulos (drums), and Jackie Miclau (keyboards, guitar).
